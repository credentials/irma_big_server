Adding professional medical attributes from the BIG-register

The Dutch BIG-register contains data about health professionals who are registered in The Netherlands, in particular their professions and registration numbers. In case you occur in the BIG-register, you can load these attributes from the BIG-register into your IRMA app. This allows you to prove to others, for instance during login, that you exercise a certain medical profession, including your registration number.

This requires two things:

  • You are at this moment actually registered in the BIG-register.
  • U have installed the IRMA app and you have loaded at least the iDIN attributes.

In order to obtain attributes from the BIG-register, you first need to disclose a number of attributes about yourself, namely your name, date of birth, and gender. The Privacy by Design foundation uses these data to search the (public) BIG-register and check if you occur in it. If an unambiguous and valid registration is found, the foundation can issue to your IRMA app the following BIG-attributes: your profession, your BIG-number, and your medical specialisation(s). Subsequently, the foundation removes all your data from its systems. If you do not occur in the BIG-register, no attributes are issed.
